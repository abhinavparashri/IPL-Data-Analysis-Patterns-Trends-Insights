The IPL Data Analysis project is designed to uncover meaningful insights from match‑level and ball‑by‑ball datasets. It begins with collecting data from reliable sources such as Kaggle, followed by cleaning processes like removing duplicates, handling missing values, and standardizing team names to ensure consistency. Exploratory analysis is then performed to study toss impact, batting phases, leading scorers, top bowlers, venue biases, and season‑wise scoring trends. The analysis reveals that toss winners succeed in only about 52% of matches, showing a limited advantage. Phase‑wise study highlights that death overs contribute nearly 34% of total runs but only 18% of wickets, proving their decisive role in match outcomes. Batting analysis showcases Virat Kohli’s consistency with over 6,500 runs, while AB de Villiers maintains a strike rate above 150. Bowling analysis identifies Lasith Malinga with more than 170 wickets and Rashid Khan’s economy rate of 6.5 runs per over. Venue studies reveal scoring biases, with Chinnaswamy Stadium averaging 180 runs per match. Season trends confirm IPL’s evolution into a higher‑scoring league. The project stands out by combining rigorous data cleaning, advanced visualizations such as radar charts and heatmaps, and storytelling, culminating in a surprising insight: death overs economy is more decisive than Powerplay runs.
